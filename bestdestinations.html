Japan

The imposing and holy Mount Fuji awakens in the sunlight. The mysterious waves of mist rise up from the green rice fields. Meanwhile the life in cities such as tokyo continue undisturbed. From busy city blocks to popular shopping malls with the latest technological gadgets and the seren temples around the country. It is a place full of beauty and history worth visiting at least once in your life.

Jamaica

"Get up, Stand up!" During your holiday in Jamaica you will experience the relaxed atmosphere and view the impressive sights on the island, but also the friendly Jamaicans and their 'easygoing' way of life. They turned their relaxed and easygoing personality into their number one export product. Enjoy your sun-drenched beach holiday here under swaying palms while listening to the cheerful reggae music in the background. Welcome to Jamaica!

Morocco

Our holidays take you to the Atlantic coast, as well as blush-coloured Marrakech and the Atlas Mountains. With more than eight million tourists come to the country every year in search of a beach break with a twist. Most people choose Agadir as their base, and for good reason. The stretch of coast here unravels for six miles and basks in 300 days of sunshine a year. Having all these beautiful cities to visit with the warm sun and delicious food, you cant miss your chance to visit Morocco.

Sri Lanka

Feel the hospitality during your holiday in Sri Lanka and experience the rich history and culture of this fascinating country in Southeast Asia. The dense green jungle here is beautifully interspersed with extensive tea plantations combined with the beautiful Buddhist monasteries and temples full of colorful frescoes. Are you in need of relaxation during your holiday in Sri Lanka? Thanks to the long coastline, you can enjoy a different beach every day!

Mauritus

At first glance, Mauritius looks like a close relative of the Caribbean Islands. Around 1,200 miles from the east coast of Africa, the island’s hemmed by 330 kilometres of immaculate white beaches. Take to the waters, meanwhile, and you'll spot pods of dolphins playing. The south, west and east coasts are defined by top-drawer beaches – one of the longest stretches of sand is Belle Mare, which borders a coral-protected lagoon. In the north, the shallow bays at Balaclava and Calodyne give themselves over to watersports like snorkelling and kite-surfing.

Abu Dhabi

Abu Dhabi’s more like a half-sibling than an identical twin to Dubai. It’s grown just as quickly, with the head count jumping from 40,000 in the 1960s to 2.9 million today. Skyscrapers like the Capital Gate and the Etihad Towers are cut from a similar cloth to those in Dubai, and the Abu Dhabi Mall mimics its swanky shopping scene. But unlike Dubai, this place is loaded with authentic Emirati culture, so it’s easy to team modern sights with a dose of history.
